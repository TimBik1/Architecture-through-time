Bartolomeo Rastrelli

Francesco Bartolomeo Rastrelli was a prominent Italian architect who worked in Russia. Known for the iconic Winter Palace in St. Petersburg, he played an essential role in introducing the Baroque style to Russia. Rastrelli's designs are characterized by elaborate ornamentation, bold colors, and the integration of gardens and palace complexes, creating an opulent spectacle that captivated visitors and royalty alike.
